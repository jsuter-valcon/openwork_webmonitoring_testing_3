How Does Moving House with a Mortgage Work in the Bristol Area?

Introduction: If you're considering moving house while having an existing mortgage, it's important to understand how the process works. In this blog post, we will provide you with trustworthy and honest insights into moving house with a mortgage, including options such as porting your current mortgage or finding a new deal by re-mortgaging. At Strong Mortgage Solutions, we aim to guide you through this process and help you make the best decisions for your situation.


Porting or Remortgaging: When moving house, you have two main options for your mortgage. The first option is to port your current mortgage, which means transferring it to your new property. This can be done with your existing lender, and it allows you to keep your current mortgage deal. However, it's important to note that the lender will assess whether the new property meets their criteria as suitable security. If it doesn't, you may need to explore other options.


The second option is to remortgage, either with your current lender or a different one. This involves finding a new mortgage deal that suits your needs. Keep in mind that if you choose to remortgage, there may be early repayment charges (ERC) associated with ending your current mortgage deal. At Strong Mortgage Solutions, we analyse various scenarios to determine the best option for you, ensuring it aligns with your plans and minimizes any potential financial costs.


Borrowing Additional Funds: If you need to borrow more money to purchase your new home, you will typically need to apply for another mortgage. This additional borrowing can be obtained from your current lender or a different one, depending on your circumstances. It's important to note that the new borrowing will be subject to the rates applicable at the time of application. This means your payment amount may differ from your current rate, and additional costs like arrangement fees or valuation fees may apply.


Financial Assessment and Credit History: When applying for a new mortgage or porting your existing one, your lender will assess your credit history and financial position. This is to ensure that you meet their criteria and can afford the mortgage repayments. At Strong Mortgage Solutions, we assist you in understanding your credit history and financial situation, putting you in the best position to secure the right mortgage for your new home.


Payment and Completion: Throughout the process of moving house, you will continue paying your current mortgage until the completion of your new home. Once the new property purchase is finalised, your new mortgage payments will begin. It's important to budget for additional costs such as estate agent fees and conveyancing fees, which need to be paid before the new mortgage commences.


Conclusion: Moving house with a mortgage involves careful consideration of your options. Whether you choose to port your current mortgage or remortgage, it's essential to work with a trusted mortgage advisor who can guide you through the process. At Strong Mortgage Solutions, we provide trustworthy and honest advice, helping you navigate the complexities of moving house with a mortgage. Contact us today to discuss your specific situation and take the next steps toward a successful move to your new home.

Postal Notifications: To continue receiving important mail, remember to update your address with companies that regularly send you post. This includes banks, loan companies, and any other institutions you have financial ties with. Ensuring they have your new address will help you stay connected and avoid any potential issues.


Local Authority and Council Tax: If you're moving to a different council area, it's essential to register with the local authority and update your council tax information. This ensures that you're properly registered for tax purposes and avoids any complications down the line. Government and Financial Institutions: There are several government-related organisations that should be notified of your change of address. If you're self-employed, don't forget to update your company registration address with Companies House. Lastly, inform your banks and financial institutions to keep them updated and ensure a seamless transition in managing your finances.

By following this trustworthy and informative checklist, you can stay organised and avoid any disruptions in receiving important mail or managing your accounts. Our team can provide guidance and explore the feasibility of this option, ensuring it aligns with your specific circumstances.

Securing a New Mortgage: Alternatively, you may choose to secure a new mortgage with a different lender for your new property. This involves paying off your old mortgage and taking out a fresh loan that accounts for any outstanding costs. Our mortgage experts are equipped to assist you in finding a new lender and securing a suitable mortgage deal tailored to your needs.


Navigating the Next Steps: Once you have accepted an offer on your current home and had an offer accepted on your future property, we can guide you through the next steps to secure your new mortgage. Our team will provide comprehensive support, addressing any questions or concerns you may have along the way. We are dedicated to being there for you until you receive the keys to your new home.


Personalised Advice and Assistance: We understand that every situation is unique, and that's why we offer personalised advice and assistance throughout the moving process. Our knowledgeable team is available to address any queries you may have, ensuring you feel informed and confident as you make important decisions regarding your mortgage.
