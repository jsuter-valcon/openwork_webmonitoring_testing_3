  • Equity Release Council Affiliated Lenders: At Strong Mortgage Solutions, we prioritise working with lenders who are members of the Equity Release Council. The Equity Release Council is an industry body that sets high standards and promotes consumer protection within the equity release market. Lenders associated with the Equity Release Council adhere to a code of conduct, offering certain guarantees to borrowers.

One such guarantee is the "no negative equity guarantee." This means that if the outstanding equity release mortgage balance exceeds the value of your property when the mortgage is repaid (e.g., upon death or entering long-term care), your estate or beneficiaries will not be held responsible for the shortfall. The lender will only use the property value to settle the outstanding debt, ensuring that no debt is passed on to your loved ones.

  • Non-Affiliated Lenders: While there are lenders who offer equity-release mortgages but are not members of the Equity Release Council, it's important to be cautious when considering them. Choosing a lender outside the Equity Release Council means you may not benefit from the same level of consumer protection. In such cases, the lender's terms and conditions regarding negative equity guarantees may differ, potentially leaving your estate or beneficiaries with outstanding debt.

If you opt to pay the interest payments on your equity release mortgage, you have the advantage of controlling the growth of your mortgage balance. By making regular interest payments, you can prevent compound interest from accumulating. It's important to understand that choosing compound interest means leaving less equity to your estate for your beneficiaries. As interest accrues, it gradually eats into the remaining equity in your property. Therefore, if preserving a significant portion of your equity for inheritance purposes is a priority, paying the interest payments may be a more suitable option.

Equity release mortgages, often referred to as lifetime mortgages, are designed to allow homeowners to access the equity tied up in their property while retaining the right to live in their home. These mortgages are typically intended for individuals aged 55 and above. At Strong Mortgage Solutions, we work exclusively with lenders who are members of the Equity Release Council. This membership ensures that the equity release mortgages or lifetime mortgages we offer come with a no-negative guarantee. This means that in the event of your passing, your estate will not be burdened with any outstanding debt beyond the value of your property. This safeguard provides peace of mind to homeowners, knowing that their loved ones will not be left with a financial liability.


On the other hand, lifetime mortgages are a specific type of equity-release mortgage. They involve borrowing against the value of your home and receiving a lump sum or regular income, while still maintaining ownership of your property. The borrowed amount, along with the accrued interest, is repaid when the property is sold, typically upon the homeowner's passing or when they move into long-term care.


It's important to note that not all equity-release mortgages fall under the category of lifetime mortgages. There are other types of equity release products available, such as home reversion plans, where you sell a portion or all of your property in exchange for a lump sum or regular income. Home reversion plans differ from lifetime mortgages as they involve selling a share of your property rather than borrowing against it.
